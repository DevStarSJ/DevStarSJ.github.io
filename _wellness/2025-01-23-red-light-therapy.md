What if shining specific wavelengths of light on your body could reduce inflammation, improve skin, and speed recovery?

## What is Red Light Therapy?

Red light therapy (RLT), also called photobiomodulation, uses specific wavelengths of red and near-infrared light to penetrate skin and tissues, triggering cellular responses.

> "Light is a drug. The right wavelengths, at the right dose, can be therapeutic."

## The Science: How It Works

### Mitochondrial Activation
Red and near-infrared light are absorbed by **cytochrome c oxidase** in your mitochondria, boosting ATP (energy) production.

### Key Wavelengths
- **Red light (630-660nm)**: Penetrates skin, great for surface-level benefits
- **Near-infrared (810-850nm)**: Penetrates deeper into muscle, joints, and organs

### Cellular Effects
- Increased ATP production
- Reduced oxidative stress
- Improved blood flow
- Decreased inflammation
- Enhanced collagen production

## How to Use Red Light Therapy

### Distance
- **6-12 inches** from the device for most benefits
- Closer for skin, slightly farther for deeper penetration

### Duration
- **10-20 minutes** per area
- Daily use is safe and often recommended

### Timing
- Morning: Energy and mood boost
- Post-workout: Recovery enhancement
- Evening: Avoid too close to bedtime (can be stimulating)

### What to Wear
- **Less is more**: Light doesn't penetrate clothing well
- Target exposed skin for best results

## Safety Considerations

### Generally Safe For
- Most healthy adults
- Daily use at recommended doses

### Precautions
- Avoid looking directly at bright panels
- May not be suitable with certain medications (photosensitivity)
- Start with shorter sessions

### Not a Replacement For
- Medical treatment
- Sunscreen (doesn't protect against UV)
- Healthy lifestyle habits
